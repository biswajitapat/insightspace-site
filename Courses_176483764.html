​Courses Offered

​​Social Science Foundation for Civil Services

​​A course for students curious about Civil Services but not yet ready to dive in fully.
It introduces core areas — Sociology, Polity, Economy, Culture, and Geography — and helps you see how social sciences shape the exam’s questions and expectations.
The classes follow a PYQ-based, discussion-led approach that builds conceptual understanding and analytical thinking.
Perfect for arts or science students who want to explore their interest and readiness before starting full-time preparation.
Duration: 6 months | Mode: Classroom / Online

​​Semester Support (For +2, UG & PG Students)

​​Many students struggle not because they lack effort, but because theory feels distant or abstract.
This course breaks down complex ideas and thinkers into simple, relatable discussions that make sense.
Currently offered only for Sociology, it helps +2, undergraduate, and postgraduate learners gain clarity, improve writing, and strengthen conceptual understanding.
It also prepares students aiming for postgraduate and Ph.D. admissions — building the analytical and academic foundation needed for higher studies.
Duration: Ongoing (semester-wise) | Mode: Classroom / Online

​​​Teacher Preparation (B.Ed., D.El.Ed., OTET, CTET, OSTET & ITEP)

​Across India, governments are working toward two big goals — making education available to every child and improving the quality of teaching. This has created a growing need for well-prepared teachers and, in turn, a large number of students aspiring to join the profession.
Many find it difficult to secure admission into D.El.Ed. and B.Ed. programmes. Those who do often struggle with qualifying exams such as OTET, CTET, and OSTET.
This course is designed to guide teacher aspirants through these challenges with clear concepts, strategic preparation, and focused revision. It also supports students preparing for ITEP admissions.
Duration: 6 months | Mode: Classroom / Online

​The Thinking Classroom (School Students)

​The foundation for professional success is laid in school — in how a child learns to think, question, and understand the world around them.
This course goes beyond textbooks to strengthen the way students learn across English, Science, Maths, and Social Studies.
Classes are designed to build reasoning, communication, and problem-solving skills that prepare students not just for exams, but for higher studies, competitive admissions, and future careers.
For: Students of Classes 6–10 | Duration: Year-round batches | Mode: Classroom

​UGC NET–JRF (Sociology & Education)

​The UGC NET–JRF exam is entirely objective and MCQ-based, but success still depends on deep conceptual clarity and pattern recognition.
This course offers focused preparation in both Paper I and Paper II, combining core theory, question analysis, and strategy for time management and accuracy.
Classes include discussions on previous-year questions, guided revision, and regular practice tests to build confidence for the real exam.
Duration: 3 months | Mode: Classroom / Online 

​Spoken English & Communication Skills

​A practical course that helps you speak confidently and clearly in real-life contexts.
We focus on fluency, pronunciation, and the ability to think and express ideas naturally, beyond memorized dialogues.
For students, teachers, and professionals who want to grow through better communication.
Duration: 2 months | Mode: Classroom

​Summer Courses (School Students)

​​These summer batches are designed to give students an early lead before the new academic session begins.
Over two and a half months, we complete nearly half of the syllabus across core subjects — English, Science, Maths, and Social Studies.
This helps students build a strong foundation, revise key concepts, and enter school with confidence instead of pressure.
For: Classes 6–10 | Duration: 2.5 months | Mode: Classroom
